## üî• Common Objections & Responses

### "That's expensive. Instantly is only $37/mo."

**Acknowledge:**
"You're right, Instantly's platform is cheaper upfront."

**Reframe:**
"But let's break down the real cost:
- Instantly: $37
- Lead database: $97
- Your time (10h/mo at $50/hr): $500
- **Total: $634/mo + you doing all the work**

With us: $250/mo, everything done. You save $384 AND your time."

**Close:**
"The question isn't tool cost - it's opportunity cost. What's one extra client worth to you?"

---

### "I can just do it myself."

**Acknowledge:**
"Absolutely, Instantly is great if you have the time and expertise."

**Qualify:**
"Quick question: Have you run cold email campaigns before?"

**If No:**
"Then you're looking at 20+ hours learning (setup, warmup, targeting, copywriting, A/B testing). That's $1000+ of founder time. We shortcut that to 1 hour onboarding."

**If Yes:**
"Perfect! Then you know how much ongoing work it is. Reply monitoring, follow-up sequences, deliverability issues. We handle all that so you can focus on closing."

**Close:**
"Think of us as your cold email team, not a tool."

---

### "I don't believe in cold email / it's spam."

**Acknowledge:**
"I get it - most cold email IS spam. Low quality, mass blasted, irrelevant."

**Differentiate:**
"That's not what we do. We send 100/week (not 10,000), to hand-picked prospects that fit your ICP perfectly, with personalized messaging based on their pain points."

**Social Proof:**
"B2B SaaS companies doing $10M+ ARR still use cold email. The difference is quality: right person, right message, right time."

**Close:**
"Let's send 10 test emails this week. If you hate the quality, we refund 100%. But I'm confident you'll see the difference."

---

### "What if I don't get any meetings?"

**Acknowledge:**
"That's the right question. Results matter, not activity."

**Set Expectations:**
"Industry average reply rate is 2-3%. We target 5-8% with better copy and targeting. That's 5-8 replies per 100 emails."

**Qualify Success:**
"Not every reply books a meeting - some say 'not interested.' Realistic conversion: 100 emails ‚Üí 6 replies ‚Üí 1-2 qualified meetings."

**Risk Reversal:**
"If after 2 months you've gotten zero meetings, we'll refund the last month AND help you figure out why (product-market fit, pricing, messaging)."

**Close:**
"We're aligned on outcomes. Bad results = we failed you. We optimize until it works."

### "I need to think about it."

**Acknowledge:**
"Totally fair. This is a monthly commitment and you should be confident."

**Uncover Real Objection:**
"Can I ask - is it budget, timing, or something about the service you're unsure about?"

**Timing:**

**Uncertainty:**
"What would make you 100% confident? A test campaign? Call with a current client? Detailed breakdown of our process?"

**Close:**
"How about this: Let's do a 10-email test campaign next week (free). You approve the copy, we send, you see results. Then decide."

## üìû Discovery Questions (Before Pitching)

**Goal:** Understand if they're a good fit BEFORE selling.

### 1. **Qualify Their Pain**
- "What's your current strategy for getting new clients?"
- "Have you tried cold email before? What happened?"
- "What's the biggest bottleneck in your sales pipeline right now?"

**Red Flags:**
- No clear ICP ("we sell to everyone")
- Product isn't ready (pre-launch, pivoting)
- Expect instant results (want 10 clients this week)

**Green Flags:**
- Clear target market ("B2B SaaS CMOs with 10-50 employees")
- Tried cold email, failed (bad copy / no replies)
- Need consistent pipeline ("we close deals but lack leads")

---

### 2. **Qualify Their Budget**
- "What's your monthly marketing budget?"
- "How much does a new client bring in revenue (LTV)?"

**Red Flags:**
- $0 budget (expect free trial forever)
- LTV <$500 (can't afford cold email economics)
- No idea what a client is worth

**Green Flags:**
- Budget $500+/mo (can afford us)
- LTV $2K+ (strong ROI potential)
- Understand CAC:LTV ratio

---

### 3. **Qualify Their Urgency**
- "When do you need to start seeing results?"
- "What happens if you don't solve this in the next 60 days?"

**Red Flags:**
- "Just exploring" (tire kicker)
- No urgency (will ghost after demo)

**Green Flags:**
- "Need clients this quarter" (real pain)
- "Runway is 6 months, need revenue" (urgent)

- Expect 100+ meetings/month (unrealistic)

## üèÜ Closing Tips

### **Always Be Qualifying**
Don't sell to bad fits. It creates churn and bad reviews. If they're not qualified, politely refer them to Instantly.

### **Lead with Value**
Offer the 10-email test FIRST. Get them to yes on low commitment, then upsell to monthly.

### **Transparency Wins**
Don't overpromise. Set realistic expectations on reply rates, meeting conversion, timeline.

### **Assume the Close**
Don't ask "Are you interested?" Ask "When can we start the test campaign?"

### **Handle Objections with Questions**
"That's expensive" ‚Üí "Compared to what? What's your current cost of client acquisition?"
